France

Paris

Paris is one of the most beautiful cities in the world. It is known worldwide for the Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame cathedral, and the Eiffel tower. It has a reputation of being a romantic and cultural city. The city is also known for its high-quality gastronomy and the terraces of its cafés..
Must Experiences: Dine on the Eiffel Tower, take a tour of the Disneyland Paris, visit the Musée d’Orsay, shopping at Le Marais, and more.
Where To Stay: Four Seasons Hotel George V Paris, Hotel Plaza Athenee, and Shangri-La Hotel.
Best Time To Visit: June to August

Annecy

As well as its great proximity to plenty of nature, Annecy also boasts a number of green spaces, and one of the most popular spots in the city is Jardins de l'Europe (the Gardens of Europe). This charming space boasts features such as statues, mountain views, and is a great spot for a picnic!
Must Experiences: Boating on Lake Annecy, Go skiing and hiking on Mount Semnoz, cruise through the River Thiou, explore the wildlife at Parc Animalier de la Grande-Jeanne, and more.
Where To Stay: Impérial Palace, James & Lake Suites, Les Trésoms Lake and Spa Resort, and Le Boutik Hotel.
Best Time To Visit: March to May, and September to November

Saint-Paul de Venceshort

One of the oldest medieval towns on the French Riviera, it is well known for its modern and contemporary art museums and galleries such as the Fondation Maeght, and for the 17-century Saint Charles-Saint Claude chapel, which in 2012–2013 was decorated with murals by French artist Paul Conte.
Must Experiences: Canyoning on Loup River, hiking on Baou de Saint-Jeannet Mountain, explore the Bastide Aux Violettes, and more.
Where To Stay: Hôtel Le Saint-Paul, Hôtel le Hameau, La Grande Bastide, Colombe d’Or, and Hôtel Les Vergers de Saint-Paul
Best Time To Visit: April to July, and end of August to October

Reims

Visit Reims: Top 15 things to do and Must see attractions ... Bigger than Our Lady of Paris (“Notre-Dame de Paris”), it has housed the coronation of 25 kings of France since its creation and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. In total, 33 kings of France were crowned in Reims, in addition to the baptism of Clovis (first king of the Franks).
Must Experiences: Explore the Montagne de Reims Regional Natural Park, visit the oldest Porte Mars, adore the Foujita Chapel, discover the charm of Lighthouse Verzenay in Champagne – Museum of the Vine, and more.
Where To Stay: Area Les Crayères, Grand Hôtel des Templiers, Hôtel Novotel Suites Reims Centre, Holiday Inn Reims – City Centre, and Best Western Premier Hôtel de la Paix
Best Time To Visit: May to October

Calvi

Calvi, Corsica travel and tourism, attractions and ... Calvi is a small town on the north-west coast of Corsica in an idyllic location with a large sheltered bay to the front and the mountains behind, on the edge of the Balagne region. It is a popular destination with visitors because it combines a historic town and a port with the chance to enjoy a long sandy beach.
Must Experiences: Must visit the La Citadelle de Calvi, go hiking on Phare de la Revellata, hit the Calvi Beach, soak up the goodness of sun on Plage de l’Alga, and more.
Where To Stay: Hôtel La Villa, La Signoria, Hotel Regina Calvi, Hôtel Saint-Christophe, Hotel Restaurant Le Magnolia, and Hôtel Corsica
Best Time To Visit: April to July, and mid-August to mid-November
